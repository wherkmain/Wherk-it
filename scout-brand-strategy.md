# Scout - Dual-Brand Strategy (Skowt + RestockScout)

## Brand Architecture

### Primary Domain
**restockscout.com**
- SEO-focused landing page
- Captures "restock alerts" search traffic
- Clear, descriptive, trustworthy

### Product Name
**Skowt** (the app)
- Distinctive, memorable, brandable
- Becomes a verb: "skowt it", "I skowted that card"
- Alternative spelling = trademark-friendly

### Domain Strategy
- **restockscout.com** → Primary domain, landing pages, marketing
- **skowt.app** → Redirect to restockscout.com/app or use for app deep links
- **getskowt.com** → Optional redirect for verbal/word-of-mouth

---

## User Experience Flow

1. **Discovery:** User searches "how to get restock alerts for sneakers"
2. **Landing:** Lands on restockscout.com
3. **Hook:** "Never miss a drop. Skowt it instead."
4. **Onboarding:** "Create your first skowt — it takes 10 seconds"
5. **App:** Users open "Skowt" on their phone
6. **Viral:** "Just skowted the new Jordans — got the alert in 2 minutes!"

## Trademark Considerations

**Skowt**
- Alternative spelling makes it distinctive
- No exact matches found in quick search
- Recommend full USPTO search before filing
- Consider trademarking "Skowt" for software/services class

**Restock Scout**
- Descriptive terms — harder to trademark
- Use as domain/SEO strategy, not primary brand
- Don't invest heavily in "Restock Scout" as a brand name
